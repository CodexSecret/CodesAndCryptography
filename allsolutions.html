Set 4: Dialecticum

Puzzle 1: This is an 8x8 Binary Puzzle. The 0s and 1s in colored strips can be read as dots and dashes of Morse, ordered by the lyrics of Colors, by Stella Jang: “I could be Red, or I could be Yellow. I could be Blue, or I could be Purple. I could be Green, or Pink, or Black, or White. I could be any color you like.” The morse translates to "LANGUAGE".

Puzzle 2: To get the second piece of the puzzle, you can see that the image is label as 2A, so to get the second piece, you have to change it to 2B. This 8x8 magic square is from Benjamin Franklin, where the sum of columns and rows is 260. When you take the number and its character counterpart in numeric order, you get “This piece of art marks the 150th anniversary of the London Underground. (9)”, to which the answer is "LABYRINTH".

Puzzle 3: The labyrinths are all part of the Labyrinth art project for the 150th Anniversary of the London Underground. Following along, using the line indicated (each line is colored differently on the official map), you write out the answer, "BUSTLE".

Puzzle 4: This is an 8x8 grid of (John) Conway’s Game of Life. Advance it by one iteration, and the lit squares in reading order spells "GEOMETRY".

Puzzle 5: This is a 12x12 Minesweeper. The marked cells, when read in groups of 2x3, read out Braille for “The answer is APOCALYPSE.”

Puzzle 6: This is a 6x6 Aquarium puzzle. The shaded cells, when read in reading order, reads “The answer is ASSEMBLY.”

Puzzle 7: This is a 6x6 Battleships puzzle. The shaded cells, when read in order of the length, with reading order taking precedence in case of multiple ships, spells “Ans. is TOWER.”

Puzzle 8: This is a 9x9 Skyscrapers puzzle. When solved and read in the order mentioned, it reads “THE ANSWER TO THIS PUZZLE LOSES TO SPOCK AND PAPER BUT WINS AGAINST SCISSORS AND LIZARD. BIG BANG!”, a reference to The Big Bang Theory, more specifically to the game of Rock-Paper-Scissors-Lizard-Spock. The sign clued is "ROCK", the answer.

Puzzle 6: The equation is relating to Affine Cipher, and when you substitute in the numbers, you get the word "GRAPH".

Puzzle 7: The image is a photo of the moon, which is relating to the Moon Alphabet. Using the Moon Alphabet to decode, you'll get "THE PASSWORD IS GIBBOUS".

Puzzle 8: The word SKIP is bolded, which is referring to the Skip Cipher. Using the clue of 13 times, you get "THE ANSWER IS LETTERS".
